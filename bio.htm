Originally from Wisconsin, I attended the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities. I received my Bachelors of Arts with majors in German and Global studies and minors in Mass Communication and Teaching English as a Second Language.

After graduation from university I went abroad to teach English in Austria. I taught in two schools outside of Vienna at a tourism school in Semmering and a 5th-12th grade school in Neunkirchen. I taught there for one year and returned to the United States to do AmeriCorps.

I served two years with AmeriCorps in public education. My first year I spent with Minneapolis Public Schools working with the Homeless and Highly Mobile Student Services Department where I served primarily as a research assistant. The second year I served with City Year Chicago working in an 8th grade English classroom on the south side of Chicago.

Currently, I am pursuing a Masters of Science in Information Architecture at the Illinois Institute of Technology. I expect to graduate in Spring 2013.
